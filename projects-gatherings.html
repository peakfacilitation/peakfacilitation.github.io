Peak gatherings are smaller in scale but have big rewards. We work with city councils, boards of directors, leadership teams, and organizations of all sizes to design customized processes for retreats, workshops, or public meetings. If a team needs to regroup, a group needs to re-prioritize, or it’s a priority for the public to weigh in, Peak ensures that the gathering is a success.

“Unless both sides win, no agreement can be permanent.” —Jimmy Carter

Public Meetings

Engaging the public can be daunting, particularly when substantial controversy or technical complexity exists. Peak excels at delivering public meetings that are meaningful for participants and the client—without taking public comment until the wee hours of the morning. We do this through thoughtful integration of presentations, design charrettes, small group discussions, written comment, online surveys, and/or electronic polling. We have facilitated public meetings on land use projects, sustainability strategies, energy utility municipalization, parking policies, prairie dog relocation, and community vision planning.

Retreats

Boards of directors, city councils, and project teams all benefit from retreats as opportunities to step back, take stock, and make a plan for the future. Retreats often involve visioning, strategic planning, goal setting, prioritizing, and work plan construction. Retreats vary in length, but Peak always ensures that the group’s the desired outcomes are achieved. Some past retreat clients include the Boulder City Council, Colorado WaterWise, Eagle Valley Land Trust, Catamount Institute, Alliance for Sustainable Colorado, Western Resource Advocates, Friends of the Dillon Ranger District, and the High County Conservation Center.

Workshops

Organizations of all kinds encounter questions or problems that require a focused, outcome-oriented discussion by a small group. Workshops can help teams begin a new initiative, debrief a past event, or brainstorm approaches to a new project. Peak brings a variety of tools to our facilitated workshops, including visioning exercises, SWOT analysis (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats), and managed dialogue. We have facilitated workshops for directors and executive teams, cross-departmental project teams, and municipal and regional citizen and technical advisory boards.
